About Me

Hayfa Safa is a Lebanese theater artist and human rights activist who uses art as a tool for expression and social change. Her work explores themes of memory, migration, and transformation, creating safe spaces for dialogue and marginalized voices.

My work

Transit

The overlapping of circles shows that meaning is never singular; it comes from the intersection of experiences and voices. Just as the colors blend and bleed into each other, so do cultures, memories, and movements shape our identities.

My Logo

It reflects calmness, resilience, and the idea of holding vast spaces within. The pink circle speaks to tenderness, care, and vulnerability, but also to the strength of embracing difference and love in all its forms.

The logo is built around the symbol of the circle, a shape with no beginning and no end. The circle represents movement, continuity, and transformation it is at once a reminder of the cycles of life and a metaphor for journeys that never fully stop. Within the circle lives the idea of return, of migration, of a body in constant motion searching for new forms of belonging.

Each color carries its own voice and emotion. The blue circle symbolizes depth, memory, and the endless horizon of the sky. It reflects calmness, resilience, and the idea of holding vast spaces within. The pink circle speaks to tenderness, care, and vulnerability, but also to the strength of embracing difference and love in all its forms. .

The overlapping of circles shows that meaning is never singular; it comes from the intersection of experiences and voices. Just as the colors blend and bleed into each other, so do cultures, memories, and movements shape our identities.
